NEWTON COLLEGE LOWER SCHOOL

STUDENTS EXPECTATIONS & CONSEQUENCES

A.System of Positive Discipline

The focus of expectations and discipline in Lower School is on developing a growth mindset and encouraging self-reflection. We reinforce positive behaviours and discipline through the IB Learner Profile and the Newton College Attitudes to Learning.

Independent Learner:

A student who is well-organized and self-disciplined.

THINKER- a student who regularly plans his/her work carefully.

BALANCED - a student who regularly makes good use of his/her time during live lessons by managing online learning resources effectively, i.e. Google Docs, Student Dashboard, etc.

PRINCIPLED - a student who always meets classwork and homework virtual deadlines.

Creative Learner:

A student who confidently approaches learning with curiosity and audacity.

INQUIRER- a student who asks relevant, pertinent and interesting questions during live classes.

KNOWLEDGEABLE - a student who actively applies his/her learning in the virtual school environment, as proven through excellent work.

Collaborative Learner:

A student who cooperates in all learning areas and demonstrates tolerance, empathy and respect for peers.

COMMUNICATOR - a student who takes full responsibility and an active participatory role in virtual group work.

CARING - a student who demonstrates respect for others in the process of online learning by waiting for their turn and appreciating the learning processes of others.

Reflective Learner:

A student who honestly reflects on lifelong learning with confidence and commitment to meet his/her full potential.

OPEN-MINDED - a student who effectively and honestly identifies his/her strengths and weaknesses.

REFLECTIVE - a student who effectively acts on feedback for work to improve his/her learning and progress.

RISK-TAKER - a student who sets realistic targets and shows perseverance in working towards them.

B. System of Warnings and Sanctions

We firmly support the philosophy that making mistakes is part of the learning process. This includes mistakes in our behaviour and interactions with others. Only through reflection on our mistakes can we truly grow and become better people each day. Our system of warnings and sanctions is reflective of this belief and includes 4 levels:

LEVEL 1: Verbal Warning

LEVEL 2: Warning of Demerit

LEVEL 3: Demerit

LEVEL 4: Suspensión

In order for our philosophy to be successful, it is imperative that we receive full support from families in the decisions and processes that we take as a school regarding the specific details and evidence related to each individual case. Below you will find more detailed information about each level and the processes involved.

LEVEL 1: Verbal Warning:

The purpose of a verbal warning is to remind students of appropriate expected behaviours, and allow an opportunity for immediate reflection and correction. Repeated verbal warnings (3) about the same issue, will lead to a Warning of Demerit.

LEVEL 2: Warning of Demerit

The purpose of a Warning of Demerit is to engage the students in a deeper level of self-reflection by appropriately informing the parents of the student that an incident has occurred, and involving the student in reflection through a conversation with their tutor.

During this period of online learning, consequences at either LEVEL 1 or LEVEL 2 will be given for student behaviour that shows a lack of appreciation and compliance with the Attitudes to Learning that fall into these groups. Please note, this list is not exhaustive.

Independent Learner:

A Student who is well-organized and self-disciplined.

Attendance: A student who is not on time for virtual class (not arriving in the first five minutes and who has not been recognised as present by the teacher) in accordance with the subject expectations outlined by the teacher and the expectations of the school.

Cameras: A student who does not turn their camera on when asked by the teacher, unless specifically having a justification from his/her parents not to do so by completing this form

Creative Learner:

A Student who confidently approaches learning with curiosity and audacity.

Participation: A student who does not actively participate in class, showing little or no interest in learning. A student who is doing other things during class time that do not connect with the lesson (playing in class, leaving the class repeatedly etc.)

Use of technology: A student who consistently uses technology inappropriately in the online environment for example, using the chat inappropriately, using other Zoom tools without permission.

Collaborative Learner:

A student who cooperates in all learning areas and demonstrates tolerance, empathy and respect for peers.

Group work: A student who does not treat others in group work with basic respect (not listening to others opinions and ideas, interrupting others, not taking turns, etc.)

Following Directions: A student who does not follow the teacher’s directions and as a result causes interruptions or delays to the learning of others.

Reflective Learner:

A student who honestly reflects on lifelong learning with confidence and commitment to meet his/her full potential.

Readiness to learn: A student who repeatedly asks for permission or makes excuses to leave the session to collect necessary materials.

Netiquette: A student who uses inappropriate clothing in an online environment (virtual netiquette), or a student who is attending class from an inadequate location (e.g. laying on bed, floor), or who eats or drinks in class in a way that interrupts the learning process.

LEVEL 3: Demerit

The purpose of a Demerit is to engage the student and parents in reflection about a serious incident that has occurred. This process of reflection is carried out by the Head of Level in a meeting with the student and their family.

A Demerit can be issued after:

  • one or more Warnings of Demerit.
  • any serious act of indiscipline can lead directly to a demerit

Serious acts of indiscipline include the following. Please note this list is not exhaustive:

Independent Learner:

A Student who is well-organized and self-disciplined.

For participating in plagiarism or copying work.

For repeated disobedience related to not following the established rules for effective learning (such as playing online games in class time).

Creative Learner:

A Student who confidently approaches learning with curiosity and audacity.

For using a mobile phone in live class without the permission of the teacher.

For using an inappropriate online name or photo.

Collaborative Learner:

A student who cooperates in all learning areas and demonstrates tolerance, empathy and respect for peers.

For not telling the truth or being deceptive.

For any other offence deemed to seriously interfere with the learning of others by the Senior Leadership Team.

Reflective Learner:

A student who honestly reflects on lifelong learning with confidence and commitment to meet his/her full potential.

For sharing Newton Gmail account/password or any other login information with others.

For the use of offensive language.

Please note: once a Warning of Demerit or Demerit has been registered in the system, no change will be allowed.

LEVEL 4: Suspension

The purpose of a Suspension is to engage the student and parents in an extended period of reflection, and to seek additional support after a very serious incident has occurred. The period of the suspension will be determined by the seriousness of the incident on a case by case basis.

A Suspension is given for VERY SERIOUS breaches of discipline such as:

  • A serious lack of respect towards any member of the school community such as sending hurtful or aggressive messages to other members of the school community.
  • For logging into someone else’s account and disrespecting other’s login privacy
  • For sharing live class meeting IDs or links with people who are not members of his/her direct class.
  • For participating in an act of plagiarism in an evaluation situation.
  • Any act that puts into danger another member of the community.
  • Any other breach of discipline considered serious by the Lower School Senior Leadership Team.
